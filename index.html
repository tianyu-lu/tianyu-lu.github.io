About Me

Hi there! I'm a Computational Biology and Computer Science undergraduate at the University of Toronto, currently working on machine learning guided protein design with Philip Kim at the Donnelly Centre for Cellular and Biomolecular Research. Check out some of the projects I'm working on in this fascinating interdisciplinary field below.

Projects

iGEM Toronto Protein Optimization

Our attempt at a machine learning guided engineering of PETase, a plastic degradation enzyme. Includes application of transfer learning to learn sequence to function mappings and adaptation of Brookes et al.'s adaptive design method.

View Project

RNA Sequencing

Analysis of exploratory data into the role of PAX2 in kidney cell proliferation and apoptosis. Implementation of RNA-Seq pipeline from raw sequences to the identification of differentially expressed genes and their biological pathways.

View Project

iOS Development

Created three iPad apps designed for psychology experiments at the University of Toronto. Apps allow for flexible control over experiment parameters and safeguards to prevent data loss.
